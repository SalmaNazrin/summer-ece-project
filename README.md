# summer-ece-project
This repo is for understanding more about electronics 
Summer 2026 — ECE Self-Study Log

Summary

Three-week self-directed exploration across DSP, VLSI (RTL design),
and a brief evaluation of Embedded Systems, aimed at identifying a
specialization for further study and an eventual MTech in VLSI Physical
Design at IIT/IISc.

Week 1 — Foundations: Set up the development environment (Python,
Git, GitHub) and sampled all three domains through small hands-on tasks:
basic Verilog gates on HDLBits, signal generation in Python, and an
RC filter simulation in LTSpice.

Week 2 — DSP Deep Dive: Built a complete signal processing pipeline
in Python — FFT analysis, digital filtering (Butterworth low-pass/
high-pass), real audio signal processing, and spectrogram visualization
on voice, vowel, and whistle recordings.

Week 3 — VLSI / Verilog Deep Dive: Built combinational and sequential
digital circuits in Verilog — half adder, full adder, 4-bit ripple carry
adder with overflow detection, and D flip-flops with reset — simulated
and verified on EDA Playground.

Decision

DSP work was the most engaging — debugging FFT logic, building custom
filter functions, and analysing real voice data felt exploratory and
satisfying. RTL coding in Verilog, while completed successfully, felt
repetitive in comparison.

However, after researching the VLSI pipeline in more depth, I'm
committing to VLSI Physical Design specifically — the floorplanning,
timing analysis, and physical layout side of chip design — rather than
RTL coding. This is a more visual, optimization-driven discipline closer
to electrical/physical reasoning than software-style coding, and it
aligns with my interest in tackling complex, structured problems.

Next Steps
Set up OpenROAD / OpenLane (open-source Physical Design flow) during
the upcoming semester
Continue strengthening Verilog/RTL fundamentals as the necessary
foundation for Physical Design work
Target a VLSI-focused summer internship after 3rd year (Qualcomm,
Texas Instruments, Synopsys, or an IIT/IISc VLSI research lab)
Prepare for GATE ECE in 4th year, targeting MTech in VLSI Design at
IIT Bombay, IISc Bangalore, or IIT Delhi
